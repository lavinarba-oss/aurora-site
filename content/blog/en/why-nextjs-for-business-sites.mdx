Tilda solves 80% of the work in 20% of the time — and it really does. If you need a landing for a month to test a hypothesis, just use it.

But the moment a project gets long — and most of our clients think long from day one — Tilda becomes a ceiling:

- Templated visuals that brand the product as «one of».
- No real way to do complex interactive forms, calculators or embedded products.
- SEO and Core Web Vitals — «decent», not «great».
- Total cost of ownership grows faster than it looks.

Next.js gives us three things:

1. **Speed.** SSR, ISR, edge — real Lighthouse 95+ out of the box.
2. **Flexibility.** Any UI, any integration, any data source.
3. **Ownership.** The code belongs to the client, not the platform.

And the long game is total cost of ownership. Tilda charges forever. Next.js — built once, maintained by changes.
